# HSRA Outreach Email Set

**Last Updated:** 2026-03-10

## Purpose

This file provides a concise outreach email set for the HSRA / PopHealthMap motion defined in `HSRA-marketing-and-sales-plan.md`. The emails follow the repo's current strategy: academic-credibility first, relationship-led language, public-interest framing, all-state launch coverage with tailored message lanes, and commercial escalation only after fit is confirmed.

## Messaging Guardrails

- lead with Dr. Thad Perry's public-health and academic perspective, not a software pitch
- use `.edu` credibility as a trust and legitimacy signal, not as a spam-filter claim
- frame early outreach as `research partnership`, `pilot collaboration`, or `Academic Health Department-style collaboration` where appropriate
- avoid procurement-triggering language in first touch; do not lead with pricing, implementation scope, or vendor language
- keep first-touch emails short enough to scan quickly on a state-agency inbox
- spell out `Health and Social Risk Assessment` on first use so recipients do not confuse HSRA with HRSA
- connect HSRA to current buyer pressure: county/community metrics, rural health transformation, CHNA/CHA/PHAB, cancer-control, or EJ screening gaps
- use these emails as one channel inside a broader motion that also includes calls, LinkedIn touches, webinar invitations, and content reinforcement

## State Message Lanes For All-State Launch

Map first-touch outreach to the most visible state trigger instead of using one generic email for every state.

| Message lane | Best-fit use |
| --- | --- |
| Rural transformation / Medicaid pressure | States facing county/community measurement pressure tied to rural health or Medicaid transformation |
| CHA / CHIP / CHNA / PHAB planning | Local/public-health planning, accreditation, or community assessment work |
| EJ and environmental-health screening | States with clear environmental-health or post-EJScreen screening needs |
| Cancer-control / screening prioritization | Cancer-control, epidemiology, or screening-resource targeting conversations |

## Email 1 - First Touch To State Health Or Rural Health Lead

**Use when:** Opening a relationship-oriented conversation with a state health department, rural-health office, Medicaid transformation team, or epidemiology/public-health planning lead.

**Suggested subject options**

- Brief question on county/community health prioritization
- Tennessee public-health prioritization model - quick conversation?
- Exploring a research partnership around county-level health targeting

**Draft**

Hello [Name],

I am a public-health researcher at Tennessee Tech working on Health and Social Risk Assessment work focused on county- and tract-level prioritization for public-health decision-making.

We have been developing a Tennessee-first approach that combines environmental burden, health burden, and social determinants of health into one auditable view that can help answer questions such as where interventions or screening resources should go first.

I am reaching out in a research and collaboration spirit, not as a formal procurement inquiry. If this topic is relevant to your current priorities, I would value a brief conversation to learn how your team is approaching county/community-level measurement and prioritization.

If helpful, I can also share a short summary of the Tennessee framing we are using.

Best regards,

Dr. Thad Perry
Tennessee Tech University
`tperry@tntech.edu`

## Short Lines The Team Can Reuse

- We are reaching out in a research and collaboration spirit, not as a formal procurement inquiry.
- We are focused on how agencies make more defensible county/community prioritization decisions.
- Our Tennessee work is an anchor story, not a claim of nationwide deployment.
- The value is not another dashboard. It is an auditable prioritization framework.
- If this is not your lane, I would appreciate being directed to the right public-health planning or data lead.

## Avoid In First-Touch Outreach

- pricing language
- procurement or contract language
- claims that HSRA is already deployed nationwide
- claims that `.edu` guarantees inbox placement
- vague AI slogans without a concrete public-health decision question

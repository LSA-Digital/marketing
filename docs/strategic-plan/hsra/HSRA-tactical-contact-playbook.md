## Core Operating Rule

HSRA is a public-interest, academically credible, state-first outreach motion. The opening question is not "who can buy software fastest?" It is "which official route, named leader, or collaborator is most likely to route the HSRA discussion into the right state conversation?"

Default route hierarchy for the HSRA campaign:

1. `website contact form or official public inbox first`
2. `LinkedIn second`
3. `verified direct email third`
4. `calling fourth`

Do not assume one generic pitch will work across all 50 states. Each state must have a message lane tied to documented priorities, programs, pressure points, or public-health planning needs.

## HSRA Messaging Guardrails

- lead with Dr. Thad Perry's public-health and academic perspective, not a software pitch
- use `.edu` credibility as a trust signal, not as a spam-filter claim
- frame early outreach as `research partnership`, `pilot collaboration`, or `Academic Health Department-style collaboration` where appropriate
- avoid procurement-triggering language in first touch
- keep first-touch notes short enough for state-agency scanning
- spell out `Health and Social Risk Assessment` on first use
- connect the message to the state's real pressure: rural transformation, planning, EJ screening, cancer-control, or community-benefit targeting

## State Message Lanes

| Lane | Best-fit use | First-touch objective |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `S1` | rural transformation / Medicaid pressure | identify the owner for county-community measurement and prioritization |
| `S2` | CHA / CHIP / CHNA / PHAB planning | open a planning and assessment conversation |
| `S3` | environmental health / EJ screening | position HSRA as an integrated burden-screening and targeting tool |
| `S4` | cancer-control / screening prioritization | connect HSRA to population targeting and resource allocation |
| `S5` | academic or university collaboration | build a warm route into the state through research and public-health collaboration |
| `S6` | hospital or community-benefit planning | reach CHNA and community-investment stakeholders |
| `S7` | commercial handoff after fit is confirmed | move from credibility-led outreach into scope, pilot, and proposal work |

## Account Types To Research Within Each State

| Account type | Best first roles | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| state health department | state health officer, deputy, planning, epidemiology, equity | primary agency route |
| rural health or Medicaid transformation office | rural health lead, program lead | strong fit for rural transformation and funding pressure |
| environmental health or equity office | equity director, EJ lead, program director | strong fit for screening and burden lanes |
| university or public-health collaborator | dean, institute lead, faculty program lead | credibility and relationship leverage |
| local health department | planning, CHA, CHIP, PHAB, assessment leads | direct planning-use case route |
| hospital or community-benefit team | CHNA lead, community benefit director | non-agency route with strong planning pressure |

## Channel Templates

### Official route or public inbox first touch

```text
Subject: Brief question on [State] [priority] and HSRA

Hello,

I am a public-health researcher at Tennessee Tech working on Health and Social Risk Assessment work focused on county- and tract-level prioritization for public-health decision-making.

I am reaching out because [State or Agency] appears to be working on [documented priority], and that looks closely related to the type of decision support HSRA is designed to support. We are reaching out in a research and collaboration spirit, not as a formal procurement inquiry.

If this belongs with a planning, rural health, equity, epidemiology, or community-benefit owner, I would appreciate being pointed in the right direction.

Best regards,
Dr. Thad Perry
Tennessee Tech University
```

### LinkedIn connection note

```text
Hi [First Name] - reaching out because your role in [planning / rural health / equity / epidemiology / community benefit] looks closely related to the HSRA discussion we are running for [State].
```

### Direct email for named contact

```text
Subject: [State] question on county and community prioritization

Hello [Name],

I am reaching out because your role at [Organization] appears relevant to [documented state priority]. HSRA is designed to help teams make more defensible county- and tract-level prioritization decisions using an auditable framework that combines environmental burden, health burden, and social risk indicators.

If this is in your lane, I would value a brief conversation. If not, I would appreciate being pointed to the right owner.

Best regards,
Dr. Thad Perry
```

### Intern follow-up or scheduling note

```text
Subject: Following up on Dr. Perry's note

Hello [Name],

I am following up on Dr. Perry's recent note in case it would be helpful to route or schedule a short conversation.

The focus is how [State or Organization] is currently handling county/community prioritization, planning, screening, or resource-targeting questions.

Thank you,
[Intern Name]
on behalf of Dr. Thad Perry
```

### Calling script

```text
Hello, this is [Name] calling on behalf of Dr. Thad Perry at Tennessee Tech. We are trying to reach the best person for [planning / rural health / equity / epidemiology / community benefit] work related to county and community prioritization. We are not calling about a generic vendor blast. Who is the best person or office for that topic?
```

### Post-call or post-meeting follow-up

```text
Subject: Thank you - next step on HSRA discussion

Hello [Name],

Thank you again for the conversation.

The main takeaway on our side was that [State or Organization] needs a more defensible way to look at [county metrics / tract prioritization / combined environmental and social burden / resource targeting]. That is exactly the question HSRA is designed to support.

If helpful, we can prepare a short follow-up briefing focused on your context.

Best regards,
Dr. Thad Perry
with Mike Idengren
```

## Reusable Lines

- We are reaching out in a research and collaboration spirit, not as a formal procurement inquiry.
- We are focused on how agencies make more defensible county and community prioritization decisions.
- Our Tennessee work is an anchor story, not a claim of nationwide deployment.
- The value is not another dashboard. It is an auditable prioritization framework.
- If this is not your lane, I would appreciate being directed to the right planning or data lead.

## Avoid In First-Touch Outreach

- pricing language
- procurement or contract language
- claims that HSRA is already deployed nationwide
- claims that `.edu` guarantees inbox placement
- vague AI slogans without a concrete public-health decision question

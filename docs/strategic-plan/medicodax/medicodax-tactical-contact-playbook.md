## MEDICODAX Outreach Positioning

Use these core facts consistently.

- MEDICODAX is a `strategic human-AI medical coding and workflow asset`, not a broad SaaS launch.
- The strongest proof points are `human final decision control`, `auditability`, `workflow adaptation`, and `multi-EHR / FHIR-oriented architecture`.
- Both `outpatient` and `home health care` are viable landing zones.
- The preferred transaction model is `asset sale + post-close implementation SOW` for LSA Digital.
- Retail is optional upside, not the first commercial story.

## Route Selection Rules

Use the route that matches the target's job in the deal path, but keep the outreach order fixed:

1. `LinkedIn first` when a named individual exists.
2. `Website contact form second` when the named LinkedIn route does not move or no named route is verified.
3. `Email last` only when it is public, relevant, and justified as a fallback.

- `Direct strategic target` -> start with LinkedIn to the most relevant named operator, product, or strategy lead.
- `Association or ecosystem channel` -> start with LinkedIn to the named business, membership, education, or partnership leader, then use the institutional form or routed inbox.
- `Niche advisor` -> start with LinkedIn to the named managing director, founder, or business-development lead, then use the official contact route.
- `Backup broker` -> use only after direct strategic and niche advisor paths are already active, and still follow LinkedIn first when a named broker is public.

## LinkedIn And InMail Strategy

LinkedIn should be the formal first lane in the MEDICODAX outreach system, not an afterthought.

- Use `LinkedIn first` for named individuals whenever a credible named route exists.
- Use `contact form second` when the company routes inquiries centrally or the first LinkedIn touch does not move.
- Use `email last` only when the address is public, relevant, and the target remains strategically important.
- Do not spend InMails on generic company pages when a named person is known.

### When LinkedIn is the right move

- `Direct strategic targets`: use LinkedIn to reach strategy, partnerships, product, technology, RCM, coding, or M&A leaders.
- `Associations`: use LinkedIn to reach business-solutions, partnership, education, conference, chapter, or membership leaders.
- `Advisors`: use LinkedIn for named managing directors, founders, heads of healthcare, and strategic partnership leads.
- `Backup brokers`: use LinkedIn only after the primary relationship channels are already active, or when a specific broker is clearly more relevant than the general intake route.

### LinkedIn sequencing

1. Send the LinkedIn connection request, DM, or InMail first.
2. Wait `3-4 business days`.
3. If there is no reply and a verified form exists, submit the approved website-contact-form message.
4. If there is still no reply after the form step and a verified direct inbox exists, use email as the final fallback.
5. If there is no valid fallback route, pause and enrich the target record instead of stacking weak touches.

### Premium InMail allocation

With `100 InMails / month`, use them like this:

- `60` for direct strategic targets and relationship-rich consultancies
- `25` for associations, ecosystems, and event-programming contacts
- `15` for niche advisors and strategic-buyer introducers

Do not spend InMails on low-fit backup brokers until the first three buckets are already active.

- Pick one narrative per target: `strategic acquisition fit` or `ecosystem introduction / visibility`.

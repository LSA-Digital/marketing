# [Product] Tactical Contact Playbook Template

**Last Updated:** 2026-03-12

Use this template when a product needs a 50-state or multi-jurisdiction outreach motion. Organize the playbook by state first, then by account, then by contact route. Do not build one flat target table that mixes unrelated states and agencies.

## Purpose

This document translates the `[Product]` strategy into state-by-state contact tactics. It should answer:

- which states move first and why
- which account types matter inside each state
- which route to use first for each account
- what to say by state message lane, channel, and role
- when to escalate from official route to LinkedIn to email to calling

## Core Operating Rule

- Treat the `state` as the parent account and all agencies, universities, hospitals, and leaders as child records.
- Default outreach hierarchy for public-sector motions: `website contact form or official public inbox first -> LinkedIn second -> verified email third -> calling fourth`.
- Use one `message lane` per first touch. Do not stack multiple problem statements into the same opening note.
- Anchor state-specific messaging to documented priorities, plans, grants, dashboards, or leadership statements. Do not guess a state's pain points.

## State Prioritization Framework

Rank states before building outreach sequences.

| Tier | Use | Typical treatment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `Tier A` | highest-priority active pursuit states | full research, named-contact buildout, tailored multi-channel sequence |
| `Tier B` | fast followers | lighter research, first-touch coverage, promoted when signal improves |
| `Tier C` | broad-launch states | minimal but credible launch coverage, nurture until a trigger event appears |

## State Message Lanes

Map each state to its clearest first-touch lane.

| Lane | Best-fit use | First-touch angle |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `S1` | rural transformation / Medicaid pressure | county-community measurement, funding pressure, rural access |
| `S2` | CHA / CHIP / CHNA / PHAB planning | auditable prioritization for planning, accreditation, and assessment |
| `S3` | environmental health / EJ screening | integrated burden screening and targeting |
| `S4` | cancer-control / screening prioritization | resource allocation, disparity targeting, and population risk |
| `S5` | academic or university collaboration | research partnership, public-health collaboration, or state-facing credibility |
| `S6` | hospital / community-benefit planning | CHNA, community investment, and county-tract prioritization |
| `S7` | commercial handoff after fit is confirmed | scope, pilot, implementation, and proposal framing |

## Contact Role Taxonomy

Track people by function, not title alone.

| Functional role | Common title examples | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `state health officer` | commissioner, secretary, director | executive sponsor and political authority |
| `deputy or chief of staff` | deputy commissioner, chief of staff | operational gatekeeper |
| `population health or planning lead` | community health director, planning director | likely owner of planning use case |
| `equity or environmental health lead` | health equity director, EJ program lead | strong fit for burden and screening lanes |
| `informatics or epidemiology lead` | chief data officer, epidemiology director | analytics and implementation route |
| `rural health or Medicaid transformation lead` | rural health director, transformation lead | strong fit for funding-pressure lanes |
| `university or public-health collaborator` | dean, institute lead, faculty program lead | trust-building and warm-entry route |
| `hospital or community-benefit lead` | CHNA lead, community benefit director | non-agency route with strong planning pressure |
| `procurement or contracts contact` | procurement officer, contracts manager | later-stage execution and buying path |

## Messaging Guardrails

- lead with the decision problem, not vague AI language
- use institutional credibility as a trust signal, not as a deliverability claim
- avoid procurement or pricing language in first touch
- keep first-touch copy short enough for agency or university inbox scanning
- ask for the right owner if the recipient is not the best route
- keep all first-touch claims auditable and source-backed

## Channel Templates

### 1. Website contact form or official public inbox

**Use when:** The state or agency publishes an official routing form or public program inbox and there is no clear warm named route yet.

```text
Subject: Brief question on [state priority] and [product]

Hello,

I am reaching out because [State or Agency] appears to be working on [documented priority or initiative], and that looks closely related to the problem [Product] is designed to support.

[Product] helps teams [one-sentence outcome] using [auditable proof point or methodology]. We are reaching out in a practical, research-oriented spirit to understand whether this topic belongs with your team or whether there is a better owner for [message lane].

If helpful, we can share a short summary tailored to [State].

Best,
[Name]
```

### 2. LinkedIn connection note

**Use when:** A named leader exists and LinkedIn is an appropriate second route after official routing or a strong parallel route for a Tier A state.

```text
Hi [First Name] - reaching out because your work on [state priority or function] looks closely related to the [message lane] discussion we are running for [Product].
```

### 3. Direct email to named contact

**Use when:** The address is public and relevant, and the route hierarchy allows email.

```text
Subject: [State] question on [message lane]

Hello [Name],

I am reaching out because your role at [Organization] appears relevant to [state priority or initiative].

[Product] is designed to support [decision problem] with [proof points or methodology]. We are not trying to force a generic pitch into every state; the question is whether [State] has a current planning, funding, equity, screening, or implementation need where this would be worth a short discussion.

If this is not your lane, I would appreciate being pointed to the right owner.

Best,
[Name]
```

### 4. Coordinator follow-up or scheduling note

```text
Subject: Following up on [Sender]'s note

Hello [Name],

I am following up on the note sent by [Sender] in case it would be helpful to route or schedule a short conversation.

The focus is [message lane] and how [State or Organization] is currently handling [decision problem]. If someone else owns this area, I would appreciate being pointed in the right direction.

Thank you,
[Name]
```

### 5. Calling script

```text
Hello, this is [Name] calling on behalf of [Organization]. We are trying to reach the best person for [message lane or state priority]. We are not calling about a generic sales blast. We are trying to route a short note about [decision problem] to the team that handles [planning / rural health / equity / epidemiology / community benefit]. Who is the best contact for that?
```

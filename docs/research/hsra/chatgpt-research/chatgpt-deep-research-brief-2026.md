# HSRA Deep Research Brief

**Last Updated:** 2026-03-09

## Purpose

This brief is the handoff context for ChatGPT web Deep Research. It is intended to support the externally heavy parts of the HSRA research package for `MKT-1`, while keeping local product/capability claims anchored in the repo-based evidence already assembled.

## What HSRA Is

Health and Social Risk Assessment (HSRA) is a tract/county-level health-and-social risk analysis product built on the LSARS HRA engine. Current internal evidence supports:

- dual EPA and CA-OEHHA health-risk calculation methods,
- HARP2-parity validation framing,
- 50-state tract-level baseline analysis using EPA data,
- SDOH overlays and compounded-risk analytics,
- Tennessee pilot framing around pollution, cancer/public-health burden, social vulnerability, and auditable resource allocation.

## What This Research Is For

The local repo work is already done for:

- source mapping,
- product summary,
- license/services packaging,
- initial competitive landscape,
- initial government market analysis,
- EPMS section draft.

Deep Research is needed only for the externally heavy questions that require broader web synthesis and stronger citation support.

## What Deep Research Should Focus On

1. competitor categories and representative vendors,
2. 50-state government demand and procurement signals,
3. federal/state rural-health funding and compliance drivers,
4. public evidence supporting Tennessee-style replication patterns,
5. external analogs for premium-data commercialization and services packaging.

## Critical Claim Boundaries

Treat these as hard constraints:

- Do not rediscover or rewrite what the internal codebase already proves.
- Do not describe premium HPR data as already part of default HSRA unless explicitly supported.
- Do not treat Tennessee pilot framing as proof of validated adoption in other states.
- Do not claim HSRA replaces every adjacent competitor category.
- Distinguish clearly between:
  - `Implemented now`
  - `Configurable with services`
  - `Premium extension`
  - `Positioning / strategy thesis`

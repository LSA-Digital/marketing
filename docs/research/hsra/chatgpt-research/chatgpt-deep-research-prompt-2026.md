# ChatGPT Deep Research Prompt For HSRA

**Last Updated:** 2026-03-09

Copy-paste the prompt below into ChatGPT web Deep Research.

---

You are helping with external research for the product **Health and Social Risk Assessment (HSRA)**.

I have already completed local repo-based analysis of the product, its codebase, internal docs, Tennessee pilot materials, and premium HPR inputs. I do **not** want you to rediscover the internal product. I want you to do the externally heavy research that strengthens competitor, market, funding, and replication analysis.

Use the uploaded brief as the primary context document.

Your task is to produce a source-linked research report that helps strengthen these four areas:

1. competitor categories and representative vendors,
2. 50-state government demand and procurement signals,
3. federal/state rural-health funding and compliance drivers,
4. public evidence supporting Tennessee-style replication patterns,
5. external analogs for premium-data commercialization and service packaging.

Critical constraints:

- Do not invent or overstate current HSRA capabilities.
- Do not describe premium HPR data as already included by default unless explicit evidence supports that.
- Do not treat Tennessee pilot framing as validated 50-state adoption.
- Distinguish clearly between:
  - Implemented now
  - Configurable with services
  - Premium extension
  - Positioning / strategy thesis
- Use explicit citations and links for all meaningful claims.

Research questions to answer:

1. What competitor categories and representative vendors are most relevant to HSRA, and what do those tools appear to do well versus not do end-to-end?
2. What current public-sector demand signals suggest that state and local agencies would need tract/county-level products that combine environmental burden, social vulnerability, and resource-allocation support?
3. What federal and state programs, mandates, accreditation frameworks, or reporting/compliance requirements are most relevant to this product category?
4. What evidence suggests that a Tennessee-style public-health allocation use case could be relevant in other states, even if adoption is not yet proven?
5. What external commercialization patterns support selling this as software plus configuration/services, and optionally premium data?

Output requirements:

- Organize the report into these sections:
  1. Executive Summary
  2. Competitor Landscape
  3. Government / Public-Sector Demand Signals
  4. Rural Health Funding / Compliance Drivers
  5. Tennessee Replication Evidence
  6. Premium Data / Services Commercialization Patterns
  7. Safe-to-Cite Claims
  8. Open Questions / Weak Evidence
- For each section, include:
  - key findings,
  - source links,
  - buyer/program implications,
  - wording that is safe to cite directly,
  - wording that is still more thesis than proof.
- Prefer official/public sources for government, funding, compliance, and programs.
- For competitors, prefer official vendor sites and credible public descriptions.
- Keep the tone analytical, not salesy.

Important: this output will be merged back into local markdown docs and then used to update EPMS, so precision matters more than breadth.

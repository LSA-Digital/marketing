# HeyGen Script: Low-budget governments need workable systems, not bigger backlogs

**Last Updated:** 2026-03-25

## Production Notes
- **Avatar:** Nelson Smith
- **Format:** direct-to-camera
- **Target length:** 60 seconds
- **Tone:** practical, calm, government-aware
- **Audience:** business; public agencies, utilities, and regulated project teams
- **Status:** draft (ready for heygen)

One thing I have seen for years is that government agencies are often expected to solve increasingly complex environmental problems without the staff or budget to do it well. In my 1993 law review article, *Lawmaker as Lawbreaker*, I wrote about that challenge, and it's still a problem today.

The answer is not simply for governments to buy more software, or hire more people.  Instead, we combine AI technology with a public-private partnership model to bridge the gap between commercial and government technology.  This helps both permitting agencies and permit requestors to see the same facts in one place, and reduce unnecessary back-and-forth

The end result is that agencies can make better decisions with the resources they actually have. That is how you can realistically reduce permit backlogs, without spending budget that state and local governments might not have.

Both permit requestors and government have a vested interest in doing more with less -- if you're on either side and want to help things move faster, let's talk.

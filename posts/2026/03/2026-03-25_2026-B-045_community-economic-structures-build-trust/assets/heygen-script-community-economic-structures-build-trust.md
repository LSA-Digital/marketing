# HeyGen Script: Community economic structures build trust better than generic promises

- **Tone:** grounded, trust-building, community-aware
- **Audience:** business; community-facing project teams, local leaders, and public stakeholders
- **Status:** draft (ready for heygen)

Communities have heard a lot of promises over the years. What they trust less and less is generic language about future benefits.

What builds more confidence is a clearer economic structure. When people can see how a project connects to local conditions, local opportunity, and benefits that can actually be followed over time, the conversation changes. The discussion becomes less abstract and more grounded in what this project means for this place.

That is one of the ideas behind LSARS. Community trust gets stronger when the benefit is specific, visible, and built to last.  Key examples are such as better health with community health navigators, or better transportation options to get to work.  If the value is measurable and tied to local reality, people are far more likely to believe it.

If you're struggling with finding the right benefits that have the most community impact, let's talk.

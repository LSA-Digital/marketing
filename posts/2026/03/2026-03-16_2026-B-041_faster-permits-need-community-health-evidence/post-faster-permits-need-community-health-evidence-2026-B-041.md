## Post
Hi, I'm Dr. Thad Perry. Permit friction is usually not just about process. It is about whether a community believes a project will improve local conditions or make them worse.

We built a community health baseline and intervention scorecard that helps project teams see what "give something back" should actually mean in a specific place.

**How it works:**
- **Baseline risk**: start with the health and social conditions already present before the permit conversation begins.
- **Need-based commitments**: focus on interventions that address the barriers the community is already living with.
- **Measurable follow-through**: turn those commitments into something government, communities, and applicants can actually review.

**Result:** permit applicants reduce avoidable friction because the conversation shifts from generic promises to targeted, reviewable benefit.

# HeyGen Script: Faster permits need community health evidence, not generic promises

## Production Notes
- **Avatar:** Dr. Thad Perry
- **Format:** direct-to-camera
- **Target length:** 45-55 seconds
- **Tone:** direct, credible, partnership-oriented
- **Goal:** permit requestors, including developers, utilities, and construction teams
- **Status:** draft (not ready for heygen)

Permit friction is usually not just about process. It is about whether a community believes a project will improve local conditions or make them worse.

We built a community health baseline and intervention scorecard that helps project teams see what giving something back should actually mean in a specific place.

We start with the health and social conditions already present before the permit conversation begins. Then we focus on commitments that address the barriers the community is already living with. Then we make that follow-through reviewable.

That changes the conversation from generic promises to targeted, measurable benefit, and it reduces avoidable friction.

In my experience over many years, trust improves when communities can see that proposed benefits are tied to real local conditions, not just broad promises.

## On-Screen Text Cues
- Permit friction is a trust problem
- Baseline risk -> targeted commitments -> reviewable follow-through
- Move beyond generic promises
- Faster path to community trust

# HeyGen Script: Permit delays are trust problems before they are process problems

**Last Updated:** 2026-03-25

## Production Notes
- **Avatar:** Nelson Smith
- **Format:** direct-to-camera
- **Target length:** 60 seconds
- **Tone:** calm, credible, non-salesy
- **Audience:** business; permit applicants, public agencies, and community-facing stakeholders
- **Status:** draft (ready for heygen)

Most people talk about permit delays as if they are just a paperwork problem. In my experience, they are often trust problems first.

When companies, agencies, and communities are all working from fragmented information, every question takes longer, every promise sounds softer, and every review cycle gets heavier. People stop reacting to the project itself and start reacting to uncertainty.

The real opportunity is to create a process where everyone can work from a clearer set of facts. When the facts are easier to see, the tradeoffs are easier to explain, and the commitments are easier to follow, decisions move faster because trust is stronger. That does not remove disagreement, but it gives everyone a more credible place to start.

If you are struggling with community trust and transparency, let's talk.

## Post
Hi, I'm Dr. Thad Perry. One of the biggest mistakes in community health is assuming a county average tells you where help should go first.

We built a Tennessee-first tract and county prioritization briefing that combines environmental burden, health burden, and social determinants of health in one auditable view.

**How it works:**
- **Layer 1 / Public baseline**: start with transparent state, county, and tract patterns using public data.
- **Layer 2 / Targeting**: look closer at neighborhoods and households when local or monthly data shows sharper risk.
- **Layer 3 / Action**: match the highest-need places to practical moves like screening, care navigation, or mobile services.

**Result:** agencies can defend why resources went to one community first, and communities can see that the decision was based on evidence instead of guesswork.

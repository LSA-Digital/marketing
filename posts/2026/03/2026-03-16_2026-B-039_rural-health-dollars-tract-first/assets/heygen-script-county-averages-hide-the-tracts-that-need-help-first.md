# HeyGen Script: County averages hide the tracts that need help first

## Production Notes
- **Avatar:** Dr. Thad Perry

- **Target length:** <= 60 seconds
- **Tone:** calm, credible, public-health first
- **Audience:** state and local health departments
- **Status:** draft (ready for heygen)

One of the biggest challenges in community health is determining where resource depoyment will have the most impact, and then following through with measurable improvements.  I have been doing this kind of public-health and informatics work for many years, and the lesson is consistent: better decisions start with a clearer view of where need is highest.

In Tennessee, my team has worked with AI experts to develop a tract and county prioritization solution that combines environmental burden, health burden, and social determinants of health in one auditable view.  First, we start with a transparent public-data baseline. Then we look more closely at neighborhoods and households when local conditions show sharper risk.  Then, we match the highest-need places to practical actions, like screening, care navigation, or mobile services.

The final step is the actual deployment of community Clicincal Care Navigators, who work directly with the communities to help them understand why it's important to get to the doctor, build healthy habits, and help them with resources to actually follow through.  The outcome is simple: health agencies can defend resource allocation decisions, and community health improvement metrics back the claim. 

Right now, we are listening to other states and local community leadership to understand unique challenges and solutions.  If you are working to improve community health outcomes, let’s talk!

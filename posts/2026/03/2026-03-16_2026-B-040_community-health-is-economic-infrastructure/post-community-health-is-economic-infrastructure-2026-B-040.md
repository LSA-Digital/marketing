## Post
Hi, I'm Dr. Thad Perry. Economic development is not just about sites, power, and jobs. If a community cannot get to care, cannot get to work reliably, or is already carrying a high health burden, growth gets harder to sustain.

We built a community health opportunity briefing that shows where health burden and access barriers overlap, so commerce teams can target incentives that strengthen local capacity instead of adding friction.

**How it works:**
- **Health baseline**: identify counties and tracts where cumulative burden is already high.
- **Barrier scan**: surface transportation, care-access, and other social-risk factors that limit workforce participation and resilience.
- **Investment fit**: connect those findings to practical moves like mobility support, care navigation, or neighborhood service deployment.

**Result:** economic-development teams get a more credible way to link growth plans to measurable community benefit.

# HeyGen Script: Community health is economic infrastructure

## Production Notes
- **Avatar:** Dr. Thad Perry
- **Format:** direct-to-camera
- **Target length:** <= 60 seconds
- **Tone:** thoughtful, practical, non-salesy
- **Audience:** state and local departments of commerce
- **Status** draft (ready for heygen)

Economic development is not just about sites, power, and jobs. If a community cannot get to care, cannot get to work reliably, or is already carrying a high health burden, growth gets harder to sustain.

After many years working at the intersection of health data, communities, and outcomes, I can tell you this: durable economic progress is much easier to sustain when community health is part of the plan from the beginning.

In Tennessee, my team has worked with AI experts to build a multi-layer community health solution that shows where health burden and access barriers overlap, so commerce teams can target investments that strengthen local capacity, where it will deliver the most impact.  We start with the health baseline, then scan for barriers like transportation and care access. Then we connect those findings to practical options, like mobility support, care navigation, or neighborhood service deployment.

That gives economic-development teams a more credible way to connect growth plans to measurable community benefit.  Right now, we are collecting pain points and feedback from states and communities to improve the approach - if you are working to improve community health and economic outcomes, let's talk!
